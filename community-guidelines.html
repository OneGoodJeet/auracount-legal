Community Guidelines

TL;DR: Be kind. Don't harass. Don't impersonate. Don't spam. Don't post illegal or sexual content. We review reports within 24 hours and we remove accounts that break these rules.

1. Who these rules apply to

These Community Guidelines apply to everyone using AuraCount's social features, including friend profiles, comparisons, leaderboards, activity feeds, ratings, recap sharing, referral codes, and any other place where you interact with other users. They are part of, and do not replace, the Terms of Service.

2. What you can do

AuraCount exists so you can understand your own listening habits and share them with people you choose. You're welcome to:

  • Create a profile with your real name, a nickname, or a handle that represents you.
  • Add friends, accept or decline friend requests, and remove friends at any time.
  • Compare stats, view shared artists, and participate in leaderboards with your friends.
  • Rate tracks and albums, and share your ratings with friends.
  • Share AuraCount-generated recap cards to other apps.
  • Send your personal referral code to people you know.

3. What is not allowed

The following content and behavior is prohibited on AuraCount. Violations may result in content removal, profile suspension, or permanent account termination without notice.

3.1 Harassment & bullying

  • Targeting another user with hateful, threatening, or demeaning messages or profile content.
  • Encouraging others to harass, dogpile, or brigade another user.
  • Sharing someone else's private information (doxxing) in a username, display name, or referral code vanity string.

3.2 Impersonation

  • Pretending to be another user, a public figure, a brand, or AuraCount staff.
  • Using a username, display name, or avatar designed to make other users believe you are someone you are not.

3.3 Spam, scams & manipulation

  • Mass friend requests to users you don't know with the intent to sell, promote, or advertise.
  • Referral-code abuse: creating multiple accounts to farm referral rewards, or trading codes on third-party sites.
  • Coordinated inauthentic behavior to inflate leaderboards, comparison scores, or rating counts.
  • Phishing, malware links, or any attempt to obtain another user's credentials.

3.4 Sexual, violent, or illegal content

  • Sexual content involving minors. This is a zero-tolerance rule. We will permanently terminate accounts and report to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) where applicable.
  • Graphic violence, gore, or content that promotes self-harm or suicide.
  • Content that promotes terrorism, violent extremism, or hate groups.
  • Content that violates any law in the user's jurisdiction or ours.

3.5 Hate speech

  • Slurs, dehumanizing language, or calls for violence against people based on race, ethnicity, religion, national origin, caste, sexual orientation, gender identity, disability, or serious disease.
  • Display of hate symbols in avatars or profile text.

3.6 Platform abuse

  • Reverse-engineering, scraping, or automating access to AuraCount or its server APIs.
  • Exploiting bugs or rate-limit gaps to degrade service for others.
  • Submitting false reports to abuse the reporting system.

4. How to report content or users

If you see a profile or behavior that violates these guidelines:

  1. Open the user's profile in AuraCount.
  2. Tap the ··· menu in the top right.
  3. Select Report User, choose a reason, and add details.

Review timeline: Our team reviews every report within 24 hours. Urgent reports involving imminent harm, minors, or threats of violence are triaged first. You will not receive individual confirmation of the outcome, but confirmed violations result in content removal and account action.

5. How to block another user

Blocking is immediate and one-directional — you do not need anyone's permission to block someone.

  1. Open the user's profile in AuraCount.
  2. Tap the ··· menu and choose Block User.
  3. Confirm the block.

Once blocked:

  • You will not see their profile, listening data, activity, ratings, or comparisons anywhere in the app.
  • They will not see yours.
  • They cannot find you through user search or leaderboards.
  • They cannot send you a friend request.

You can unblock someone at any time from Profile → Privacy → Blocked Users.

6. What happens when rules are broken

Enforcement is proportional to severity and history:

  • Warning. A one-time notice for a minor, first-time violation.
  • Content removal. Violating profile fields, usernames, or referral codes are reset to generic defaults.
  • Temporary suspension. Account features (friend requests, activity posting, leaderboards) are disabled for a period.
  • Permanent termination. The account is deleted and the user is banned from creating new ones. Applied immediately for sexual content involving minors, credible threats of violence, and repeat serious violations.

No appeals for severe violations. Accounts terminated for CSAM, credible threats, or coordinated harassment are permanent. For other terminations, you can appeal by contacting support@auracount.app within 30 days.

7. Filtering objectionable content

AuraCount does not host user-generated posts, videos, or free-form text beyond usernames, display names, review text on ratings, and referral code vanity strings. All of these fields go through automated moderation for prohibited terms before they reach other users, and each is also subject to the reporting and review process above.

You can further reduce what you see by:

  • Setting your profile visibility to Friends Only or Nobody in Profile → Privacy.
  • Turning off Show in Leaderboards and Show Listening Activity.
  • Using Block User on any account whose content you do not want to see.

8. Changes to these guidelines

We may update these Community Guidelines as the product and the community evolve. Material changes will be announced in the app and at the top of this page with a new effective date. Continuing to use AuraCount after a change constitutes acceptance of the updated guidelines.

9. Contact

Questions, appeals, or safety concerns: support@auracount.app.

For urgent safety issues involving a minor or an imminent threat, include "URGENT - SAFETY" in the subject line.
